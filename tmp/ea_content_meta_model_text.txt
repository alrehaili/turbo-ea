01. Introduction
Based on the responsibilities of the Digital Government Authority (DGA) in organizing the digital government
business and guiding government entities to implement best practices in various fields related to achieving the
National Digital Government Strategy and enabling and accelerating the pace for implementing sustainable
digital transformation for government entities in line with the ambitious Vision 2030, DGA has developed the
National Enterprise Architecture (EA) Framework to reflect the recent developments and trends related to the
field of enterprise architecture, as part of its continuous efforts to reach the highest levels of maturity in
adopting the enterprise architecture practice in government entities, national companies and other entities
seeking to do so.
The updated National EA Framework is linked to the Digital Government Regulatory Framework, as this
Regulatory Framework includes a set of policies, controls, standards and guidelines that contain the artifacts
of the National EA Framework. The National EA Framework contains a set of guidelines and controls for
implementing the national EA practice as shown in the following figure:

02. Objectives
This document aims to provide the necessary guiding artifacts to enable entities to prepare their respective EA
Content Meta Model and thus develop the entity’s EA artifacts. The following points summarize the objectives
of this document:
• Provide a clear and detailed reference for building the EA Content Meta Model at the entity according to
the best global standards and the national regulatory requirements, which can be used to configure the EA
Tool and accelerate the digitization of the EA practice.

05. EA Content Meta Model
5.1 Introduction to the EA Content Meta Model
The EA Content Meta Model represents all the building blocks used to describe the entity from an enterprise
architecture viewpoint, distributed across the enterprise architecture domains to which each building block
belongs. The following figure shows all the reference EA building blocks divided across the enterprise
architecture domains, which, together, form the landscape of EA artifacts; thus, it constitutes the starting point
for the entity to begin adjusting and developing its own model. The attributes of each of these building blocks
will be defined and detailed in the following sections of this document, in addition to the key connections
between these building blocks (the most important direct connections between the various artifacts of the
enterprise architecture domains have been defined, and additional connections may be defined as needed by
the entity).
It is common knowledge that the “strategic alignment” artifacts are not considered part of the EA domains
entrusted with the mandates of managing the enterprise architecture. However, to ensure the provision of a
comprehensive view and align EA artifacts with the strategic artifacts at the entity, a number of necessary
reference artifacts were defined in the EA Content Meta Model.

5.2 Guiding Considerations for Preparing the EA Content Meta Model
To ensure achieving the desired benefits from the EA Content Meta Model, the entity must take a set of key
considerations into account when preparing the meta model, which can be summarized as follows:
• This document should be considered a reference document only for developing the entity’s EA Metal
Model. The EA team at the entity must choose/add/adjust the building blocks according to the entity’s
needs. The EA Content Meta Model must be reviewed and updated periodically.
• The EA team at the entity shall determine the core building blocks that must be documented (mandatory
building block or optional building block) when preparing the EA Content Meta Model
• Each building block shall be defined only once within the EA Content Meta Model and shall be included
in only one of the EA domains (business, beneficiary experience, applications, data, technology,
security).
• When implementing the mandates and phases of the EA Artifacts Development Methodology, the EA
team must adhere to the EA Content Meta Model to identify the required building blocks for each scope
of work and the level of documentation (detail) required for developing the relevant enterprise
architecture viewpoints.
• The language in which the content of the building blocks and their attributes will be documented in
various domains must be taken into consideration, with the possibility of adding more than one
language to the documentation process
• Fixed values must be identified and documented for some building block attributes, to form predefined
lists (e.g. list of currencies used, date and time format, list of priorities)
• When designing EA viewpoints (a visual representation, a matrix, or a catalog of EA artifacts), it is
important to ensure that all the required artifacts and connections for the viewpoint are available. If they
are not available, the EA Content Meta Model must be updated to meet the requirements of the new
viewpoints.
• It is necessary for the EA Department to coordinate with the relevant departments at the entity to
develop the artifacts of EA domains related to its business (e.g. Enterprise Excellence Department,
Cybersecurity Department, Information Technology Department, etc.)

and nature of the entity’s business.
5.3 Details of the EA Content Meta Model
This section of the document provides a detailed definition of EA artifacts, which were linked based on the
domain of enterprise architecture to which they belong, and then were detailed as follows:
• Define the building blocks of the domain, through which a meta description is provided for each
building block of the enterprise architecture domains.
• Define the attributes of each building block, through which the attributes of building blocks are
detailed and the type of each attribute is determined.
• Define the connections specific to each building block through which the links between the building
blocks of the EA domains are identified (business, beneficiary experience, data, applications,
technology, security) and the reciprocal connections that aim to help entities build plans for EA
artifacts are identified to serve the different viewpoints and interests of stakeholders.
